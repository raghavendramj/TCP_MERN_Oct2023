They came for Virat Kohli, but they got Rohit Sharma. This was an exhibition of white-ball batting in Delhi, as India's captain turned a chase of 273 - which Afghanistan hoped would prove awkward - into a glorified middle practice, treating their seamers with the disdain usually reserved for net bowlers. India's second match of this tournament was billed as Kohli's homecoming, his second and final World Cup appearance in the city he grew up in. Twelve years ago, he made 12 off 20 balls in a low-key win over the Netherlands; now, he was the man whose name featured on every other blue jersey in the 32,000-strong crowd.
